### LTIP (Long-Term Incentive) Findings:

**Extreme Equity (70%+):**
1. DTSS: 94%
2. KITT: 88%
3. FCEL: 72%
4. PLUG: 67%

**High Equity (50-70%):**
1. ARBE: 60%
2. BLDP: 60%
3. DDD: 60%

**Moderate Equity (<50%):**
1. SIDU: 42%
2. UAVS: 39%
3. MNTS: 13%

### LTIP Structures Used:

1. **Stock Options:** Most common (PLUG, DTSS, KITT, UAVS)
2. **RSUs:** Time-vesting (ARBE, FCEL, BLDP, DDD)
3. **PSUs:** Performance-vesting (BLDP 75%, FCEL 50%, DDD)
4. **Hybrid:** Mix of options + RSUs (Most companies)

### Vesting Schedules:

- **Standard:** 3-4 years with 1-year cliff
- **Special Grants:** Immediate vesting (ARBE 185K RSUs)
- **Performance-Based:** 3 tranches tied to metrics (DDD PSUs)

### RED FLAGS FOUND:

1. **PLUG:** Performance hurdles waived (converted PSOs to time-based)
2. **FCEL:** Shareholders voted against pay increase, granted anyway
3. **MNTS:** Only 13% equity (misaligned with micro-cap norms)

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **No 200x Stock Price Targets Found** - Zero companies use explicit extreme multipliers

2. **Smaller Market Cap = Higher LTIP %**
   - <$50M: 70-94% equity
   - $50-$300M: 40-70% equity
   - $300M-$1B: 13-60% equity

3. **Founder CEOs Have Higher Alignment**
   - SIDU (Founder): 42% + ownership
   - KITT, ARBE: Large grants reflecting growth stage

4. **Performance Metrics Favor Growth Over Profitability**
   - TSR most common for PSUs
   - Stock price appreciation  
   - Revenue milestones
   - Profitability rarely primary metric for unprofitable companies

5. **Equity Vehicles Evolving**
   - Shift from options → RSUs → PSUs
   - 50/50 mix increasingly common (FCEL)
   - Market-based awards growing (DDD)
