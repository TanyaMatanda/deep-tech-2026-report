Executive Findings

We analyzed 1,687 SEC 10-K filings and found that while 98% of deep tech companies now disclose AI risks, the vast majority are using "boilerplate" language. Companies are repackaging existing Cybersecurity and Sustainability risks as "AI risks" while ignoring novel dangers like Hallucination, Alignment, and Model Collapse.

The Specificity Gap
11.4 vs 7.6

Companies average 11.4 mentions of generic terms (AI, ML, Compute) but only 7.6 mentions of specific risksâ€”and most of those are legacy risks like "cyberattack".

The Legacy Trap
74.8% vs 7.0%

74.8% of companies mention "Cyberattack" (a legacy risk) while only 7.0% mention "AI Safety" or "Alignment" (a novel AI risk).

The LLM Paradox
2,612 vs 149

2,612 mentions of "LLM" capabilities, but "Hallucination" remains a niche concern in risk factors, with only 149 mentions.
